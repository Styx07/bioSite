Barna Kiss

Barna's Story

Born in Hungary, August 28, 1912. He grew up and finished high school as a promise to his mother. Then went against family tradition and did not go to college, but left to follow his dream of sailing the world. Later he joined the Hungarian army and became an officer before World War II started. During the war he was commanded to march a battalion of 214 Jews 1500 miles to death, however, every one of them survived because of the care he gave them; other battalions were not so lucky.

Later he rented out apartments and with the help of one of the survivors from the death march battalion, who was a lawyer, stole and forged identity papers for hiding Jews and getting them out of the county and to safety.

He and his family, with many struggles, finally made it out of Hungary and made it to Israel. Later they immigtated to America and settled in Queens, New York. There he became a painter and he lived a better life with his wife and two children. He died at the age of 72 from cancer.

His wife, Henrietta, passed away in 2008. His son, Peter and his wife Karen live in Huntsville, AL, and owns a cyber security firm. His daughter, Judith and her husband Thomas are retired in Bellevue, NE.
